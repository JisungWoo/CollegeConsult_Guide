# Admissions content governance

## Evidence classes

1. **Stable principle** — durable counseling guidance, reviewed annually.
2. **School-specific conceptual fact** — educational structure or fit distinction, supported by an official institutional source and reviewed annually.
3. **Time-sensitive policy** — testing, early plans, deadlines, requirements, aid, interview, or platform details; reviewed every cycle and shortly before advising a student.
4. **Consultant judgment** — explicitly framed as a question, caution, or interpretation rather than institutional fact.

## Required policy metadata

Every time-sensitive record contains institution, category, application cycle, status, summary, official source URL/title, last verified date, next review date, and a warning to recheck the official site.

## Review workflow

1. Open the stored official source—not a blog or secondary summary.
2. Confirm the page names the applicable entering/application cycle.
3. Record the exact status in neutral language without extrapolating to a later cycle.
4. Update `lastVerifiedAt`, `nextReviewDate`, and verification status.
5. Run content validation and the policy-state tests.
6. Archive the prior record if the cycle changes; never silently overwrite historical meaning.

## Editorial prohibitions

- No guarantees, fabricated probabilities, universal score thresholds, “Ivy-ready” scores, or acceptance predictions.
- No implication that prestige is a measure of student worth.
- No manufactured activities, purchased credentials, ghostwriting, or unethical marketing claims.
- No school is labeled a safety, target, or likely if it is an Ivy League institution.
- No content or references from unrelated third-party counseling platforms.

## Launch gate

All current-cycle records must be re-opened and checked against official sources within 30 days of public launch. Records outside that window display “Verification needed.”
