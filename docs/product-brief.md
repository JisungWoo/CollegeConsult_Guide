# Product brief

## Product

**The Admissions Desk** is a field guide and working desk for thoughtful college counseling. It combines an edited training curriculum, a source-governed reference library, and privacy-first consultation tools.

## User outcome

A developing or working consultant can:

- learn selective-admissions principles without prestige myths;
- compare the eight Ivy League institutions by educational model and fit;
- evaluate readiness qualitatively without predicting admission;
- plan grades 9–12 and run structured meetings;
- practice against fictional cases;
- save local notes and print clear summaries;
- identify policy claims that require official-source verification.

## Product boundaries

- No probabilities, guarantees, admit/deny predictions, student worth scores, or admissions “hacks.”
- No ghostwriting or manufactured activities.
- No remote sensitive-data storage, analytics, authentication, billing, or third-party trackers in the first release.
- No content or product references from unrelated third-party counseling platforms.
- The product is educational and organizational, not legal, financial, mental-health, or tax advice.

## First-release acceptance

Every primary route has a working purpose; all eight school profiles are distinct; all sixteen curriculum modules are represented; comparison, roadmap, diagnostic, consult, case, search, local persistence, export/import/reset, and print flows operate; build, type, lint, tests, and browser checks pass.
